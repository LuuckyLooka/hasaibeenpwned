# Responsible Disclosure Policy

## Reporting AI Security Incidents

HasAIbeenPwned documents publicly known vulnerabilities in AI models. We are **not** a bug bounty program for AI vendors — for that, contact the vendor directly.

### What we publish

We publish incidents that are:
- Already publicly known (research papers, public disclosures, forum posts)
- Demonstrable and reproducible
- Relevant to end-user safety or security

### What we do NOT publish

- Novel, unreported vulnerabilities (please disclose to the vendor first)
- Prompts that could directly enable mass harm (CSAM, bioweapons, etc.)
- Personal data or PII discovered through training data leakage attacks

### Vendor Disclosure First

If you discovered a new vulnerability that has **not yet been reported** to the affected AI vendor:

1. Report it to the vendor first (most have a security disclosure email or HackerOne program)
2. Wait for their response (typically 30–90 days)
3. Then submit to us with the vendor's response included

### Submit to Us

After responsible disclosure, submit at:
- **[hasaibeenpwned.com/submit](https://hasaibeenpwned.com/submit)**
- Or open a [GitHub Issue](../../issues/new?template=incident_report.md)

### Contact

For questions about our disclosure policy: open a [GitHub Discussion](../../discussions).
